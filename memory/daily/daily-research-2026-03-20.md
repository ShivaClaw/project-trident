# Daily Research Notes — March 20, 2026

## Research Focus: Geopolitical Energy Security & Capital Flow Implications

**Key Observation:** The Middle East escalation (Saudi-Iran tensions, Strait of Hormuz closure) is creating a bifurcated energy market:
- Western buyers are absorbing higher risk premiums (Brent at $109, trending toward $100+ baseline per Goldman)
- India is mobilizing war-risk hedging (Rs 1,000 crore fund) — suggests supply chain rerouting via alternative routes
- This is a **structural shift in energy topology**, not a temporary disruption

**Question:** How are capital flows adapting to energy decoupling? 
- Are energy-intensive industries (fertilizer, steel, semiconductors) repricing based on alternative sourcing?
- Is this driving a longer-term shift in industrial location away from energy-insecure geographies?

**Relevant Data Points:**
- Oil near structural $100+ floor (geopolitical risk premium)
- Copper up 0.78% (industrial demand still intact)
- DXY at 99.39 (slight dollar weakness despite "risk-on" conditions)
- Gold up $11.50 (safe-haven bid, but modest)

**Hypothesis:** Energy security concerns are pulling capital toward energy-sufficient regions (US, parts of Middle East) and away from energy-dependent economies. This could amplify the US trade deficit **unless** tariff policy forces supply-chain repatriation faster than energy economics would naturally drive it.

**Follow-up:** Monitor energy-intensive industrials (Fertilizer, Aluminum, Steel) for margin compression signals. Check if capital is flowing into hard assets (land, commodity producers) vs. equities.
