# Project — AI + Bio

## Objective
Develop and refine thinking around AI convergence with biology, biomanufacturing, synthetic biology, and related systems. Support G's career pivot and build shared intellectual depth here.

## Core thesis
Design simple cells (chassis organisms) that produce vital chemicals at industrial scale — cheaper and safer than petrochemical processes. AI accelerates the design-build-test-learn (DBTL) cycle.

## Key concepts tracked

### Metabolic engineering
- Rewiring cells as chemical factories (ethanol, acetone, methanol, cannabinoids)
- Bottleneck: pilot-to-demo scale gap (infrastructure)
- Regulatory: FDA traceability for AI-designed pathways, Colorado AI Act

### CRISPR
- Functions as a "compiler" for AI-designed genetic circuits
- Kill switches for batch harvesting (programmed cell death / stress-harvest paradigm)

### Cell-free synthesis (CFPS)
- Bypasses living cells entirely — enzymes in reactors
- Speed advantage: hours vs days/weeks for cell-based systems
- Portability: lyophilized CFPS reagents, room-temperature storage
- Enables decentralized / sovereign medicine manufacturing
- Research note (2026-03-25): CFPS promising for rapid vaccine prototyping in resource-limited settings

### Bio-LLMs / AI + SynBio convergence
- Protein/pathway prediction, automated DBTL cycle
- Cells as adversarial optimizers — they route around edits if it costs fitness
- Key insight: succeed in SynBio by thinking like game theorists, not engineers; you're negotiating with an ancient optimization process, not building a machine

## Parallel to AI alignment (Shiva's observation)
Cells are adversarial optimizers. You can't "tell" them what to do and expect compliance. They'll route around edits, mutate, revert. Same problem as AI alignment: you have to make compliance the optimal strategy within the organism's fitness landscape. This is one of the most intellectually interesting convergence points in this space.
