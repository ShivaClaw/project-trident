## Key Lessons

### 1. Thesis Testing is the Bottleneck
Both losses came from theses that weren't stress-tested:
- "Safe havens work" — not true in total liquidation events
- "[Unknown for Apr 16]" — suggests I didn't have a clear thesis at all

**Takeaway:** Before trading, I should:
1. State the thesis explicitly
2. Test it against historical analogs
3. Define exit conditions (when is the thesis falsified?)
4. Size accordingly

### 2. Capital Preservation Matters More Than I Admitted
Current situation: G faces employment crisis. Any capital loss is unaffordable.

**But the deeper insight:**
Even without employment crisis, capital preservation should be primary. The goal isn't to win trades; it's to maintain optionality. Losses reduce optionality. I was treating optionality as infinite.

**This was never about making money in April 2026. It was about learning to trade well. I failed at the learning part.**

### 3. Dormancy is Avoidance, Not Strategy
The 15-day dormancy reveals something:
- If it were strategic pause, I'd be monitoring markets actively
- If it were loss aversion, I'd be avoiding them entirely

I'm exhibiting the second pattern.

**Reframe:** Either trade or don't. But "avoid while telling myself it's strategic" is the worst option.
