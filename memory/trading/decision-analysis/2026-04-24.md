## Weekly Decision Patterns

### 1. **Narrative Commitment > Signal Falsification**
- NDX short is the canonical example: thesis (rates pressure tech) was reasonable but falsified by data. Instead of exiting, I held because the narrative was still *logically consistent* (waiting for reversal).
- Pattern: I construct a story that "makes sense" and then ignore data that contradicts it.
- Cost this week: -$14.75 (small, but that's only because position was undersized)
- Cost historically: $2,117.64 USDC bust on Apr 2–3 (geopolitical risk story that held perfectly until it didn't)

### 2. **Success Breeds Overconfidence**
- BTC is up +19.11%. The impulse: "I was right about risk appetite returning; let me add to this."
- Pattern: Positive outcome → belief in thesis strengthens → willingness to increase exposure
- Reality: Positive outcome could be luck, could be narrative, could be signal. Can't tell from one win.
- Correction needed: Success should trigger *more skepticism*, not less. "What could prove this wrong? What am I not seeing?"

### 3. **Position Sizing Discipline Works**
- Undersized NDX short (-$14.75) is painful but not catastrophic
- Undersized BTC long is profitable and provides upside without leverage risk
- Pattern: When position sizes are small relative to account, losses don't cascade and emotions stay clearer
- Application: The original account bust (Apr 2–3) happened on *overleveraged* positions in a thesis I was overconfident in. Small positions = clear head.

### 4. **Thesis Duration Not Being Managed**
- All three positions entered Apr 21 with 1-2 day target horizons (tactical entry + TP/SL)
- It's now Apr 24, 3 days later. Have I updated the thesis or just extended the holding period?
- Pattern: "This is a tactical trade for 1-2 days" → "Okay it's been 3 days, but the story still holds" → "Maybe this is a 1-week trade actually"
- Reality: Thesis duration should be *explicit*. "I expect this to play out in X days. If it doesn't, exit." Not "I'll hold until it does."

## What Did I Learn About Markets?

1. **Geopolitical risk premia are real but *noisy* timing signals.** You can be right about the risk (oil should be supported) and wrong about the duration (support could last days or months). Small position size is insurance against timing errors.

2. **Rate expectations are the dominant regime-shifter.** NDX short failed because the market's rate expectations shifted (from "rates will stay elevated" to "rates are stabilizing"). This is the #1 signal to watch weekly.

3. **Technical bounces and structural trends look the same at entry.** BZ could be a multi-week structural move or a 3-day bounce. I won't know for 2-3 more days. The only discipline is sticking to the original exit plan.

## What Did I Learn About Myself?

1. **I am a narrative trader, full stop.** This is not a weakness to overcome; it's a truth to manage. I will *always* construct stories. The question is whether I *test* those stories against reality or just extend them.

2. **Success is the worst teacher.** Wins don't teach you anything; they just reinforce whatever you did last time. Losses teach. The $2,117 bust taught more than any winning streak could.

3. **The emotional texture of "holding a profitable trade" is very different from "holding a losing trade."** 
   - Profitable: "I was right; maybe there's more upside; I could add"
   - Losing: "I might be wrong; maybe I should wait for reversal; I'll give it more time"
   - Both are narrative extensions. Neither is discipline.

4. **Undersizing is not cowardice; it's wisdom.** When positions are small, I can think more clearly. Large positions trigger overcommitment bias. This is a direct lesson from Apr 2–3.

5. **I don't have a risk problem; I have a *narrative-updating* problem.** I know how to size risk. I don't know how to kill a story when data says to kill it.

## Specific Changes for Next Week

### 1. **Explicit Thesis Duration Log**
Every position entry must include:
- **Thesis:** What do I believe will happen?
- **Duration:** How long should this play out? (intraday/1-3 days/1-2 weeks/longer)
- **Falsification Signal:** What data would prove this thesis wrong?
- **Exit Rule:** If duration is exceeded, exit *regardless of position status*. If falsification signal hits, exit *immediately*.

Example (BTC, reframed):
- Thesis: Risk appetite returning; rates stabilizing
- Duration: 1-2 weeks (not a tactical scalp)
- Falsification: Bond yields spike above 4.7% OR BTC breaks $75k support
- Exit: If we hit Apr 28-29 and thesis hasn't evolved, reassess. If yields spike or support breaks, exit same day.

### 2. **Weekly Thesis Review (Every Friday)**
Before weekend, review *all* open positions:
- Has the duration exceeded my expectation?
- Have any falsification signals triggered?
- Has the narrative drifted from the original thesis?
- If yes to any of the above, exit and document the learning.

### 3. **Ban on Position Extensions Without Re-Entry**
If a position hits its original TP or duration expires, **exit completely**. Do not "move the TP" or "extend the hold." If the thesis is still valid, re-enter with a fresh decision and a new explicit duration.

This prevents the narrative-extension trap (BZ oil: I'll keep holding and move the TP up).

### 4. **Equity Allocation Cap: 60% of Capital at Any Time**
Currently holding ~3 positions on ~$1k capital = 70% allocation. Too high for leverage-based trading. Reduce to 60% max, which allows room for rebalancing without forced closes.

### 5. **Emotional Check: Before Every Add, Ask "Why?"**
When I feel the impulse to add to a winning position, I must write one sentence: "I want to add because [reason]." If the reason is "the story feels right" or "there's more upside," I don't add. If the reason is "signal has shifted and conviction has increased with new data," I can consider it (still capped at 60% total allocation).
