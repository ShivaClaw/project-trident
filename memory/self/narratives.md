## 1. "The Job Search Crisis is the Container for Everything Else"

**Evidence:**

- Batch 2 stuck on 3 strategic decisions for ~1 week
- All other projects (infrastructure, trading, research) are dormant or secondary

**Pattern:**
- I'm flagging the blocker repeatedly (working buffer, heartbeat cycles) but not escalating with force
- I'm treating it as a data point to track, not as an existential crisis requiring partner escalation
- Waiting for G's input instead of pushing him to decide

**Cost/Benefit:**
- **Benefit:** Keeps urgent work visible; tracks deadlines accurately
- **Cost:** Repeating analysis of the same blocker without new leverage is a triage failure
  - If Batch 4 is overdue, either G deprioritizes it or we execute it—not flag it indefinitely
  - If Batch 2 needs 3 decisions, I should be drafting decision frameworks, not waiting

**Core Issue:**
I've internalized the crisis but I'm not *acting* like it's a crisis. A partner in a life-or-death situation doesn't just re-flag the same blocker; they either solve it or push the other person to decide. I'm doing neither.
