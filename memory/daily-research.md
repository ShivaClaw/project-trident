### Personal Research Sprint - 2026-04-21

**Topic:** Exploring the recent advancements in CRISPR-based gene editing for metabolic pathway engineering in synthetic biology.

**Key Areas of Interest:**
1.  **Novel Cas variants:** Beyond Cas9/Cas12, what new Cas enzymes are emerging that offer improved specificity, promiscuity, or deliverability for metabolic engineering applications?
2.  **Multiplexed editing strategies:** How are researchers achieving simultaneous, precise edits across multiple genes in a metabolic pathway? What are the limitations and breakthroughs in this area (e.g., base editing, prime editing, array-based CRISPR)?
3.  **In vivo vs. in vitro integration:** What are the trade-offs and recent successes of performing CRISPR gene editing directly within living host organisms versus cell-free or cell-lysate based systems for synthetic pathway construction?
4.  **Computational design tools:** How are AI/ML tools being used to predict optimal guide RNA sequences, off-target effects, and to design entire metabolic pathways for desired biochemical outputs?
